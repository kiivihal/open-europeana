{% trans "Background" %}

{% blocktrans %}The Commission has been working for a number of years on projects to boost the digital economy. These prepared the ground for an online service that would bring together Europe's cultural heritage{% endblocktrans %}.

{% trans "The idea for Europeana came from a" %} {% trans "letter" %} {% blocktrans %}to the Presidency of Council and to the Commission on 28 April 2005. Six Heads of State and Government suggested the creation of a virtual European library, aiming to make Europe's cultural and scientific resources accessible for all{% endblocktrans %}.

{% blocktrans %}On 30 September 2005 the European Commission published the i2010: communication on digital libraries, where it announced its strategy to promote and support the creation of a European digital library, as a strategic goal within the European{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "Information Society i2010 Initiative" %}, {% blocktrans %}which aims to foster growth and jobs in the information society and media industries. The European Commission's goal for Europeana is to make European information resources easier to use in an online environment. It will build on Europe's rich heritage, combining multicultural and multilingual environments with technological advances and new business models. {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}The Europeana prototype is the result of a 2-year project that began in July 2007. Europeana.eu went live on 20 November 2008, launched by Viviane Reding, European Commissioner for Information Society and Media{% endblocktrans %}.

{% trans "Europeana is a Thematic Network funded by the European Commission under the" %} eContentplus programme, {% trans "as part of the" %} {% trans "i2010 policy" %}. {% blocktrans %}Originally SUNE known as the European digital library network – EDLnet –  it is a partnership of 100 representatives of heritage and knowledge organisations and IT experts from throughout Europe. They contribute to the Work Packages that are solving the technical and usability issues{% endblocktrans %}.

{% trans "The project is run by a core team based in the national library of the Netherlands, the" %} Koninklijke Bibliotheek. {% trans "It builds on the project management and technical expertise developed by" %} {% trans "The European Library" %}, {% trans "which is a service of the" %} {% trans "Conference of European National Librarians" %}.

{% trans "Overseeing the project is the" %} EDL Foundation, {% blocktrans %}which includes key European cultural heritage associations from the four domains. The Foundation’{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "statutes" %} {% trans "commit members to" %}:

  • {% trans "Providing access to Europe’s cultural and scientific heritage though a cross-domain portal" %}
  • {% trans "Co-operating in the delivery and sustainability of the joint portal" %}
  • {% trans "Stimulating initiatives to bring together existing digital content" %}
  • {% trans "Supporting digitisation of Europe’s cultural and scientific heritage" %}

 

{% trans "Technical plans" %}

{% blocktrans %}The development route, site architecture and technical specifications are all published as{% endblocktrans %} {% trans "deliverable outcomes" %} {% blocktrans %}of the project. After the launch of the Europeana prototype, the project's final task is to recommend a business model that will ensure the sustainability of the website. It will also report on the further research and implementation needed to make Europe's cultural heritage fully interoperable and accessible through a truly multilingual service{% endblocktrans %}.
